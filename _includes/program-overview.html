The Ghana in Data open source program is managed by the open source office at DevForce Africa. We organize a community of open source practitioners and enthusiasts to accomplish our mission of making open source software development a norm in Africa.

Expert support & Resources

A coalition of teams, experts, and friendly resources are available to make sure that everyone interested in contributing to our cause understands how to use open source.

  • Easy to Follow Guides for Existing and Aspiring Contributers: Comprehensive reference material for everyone to refer to helps to share knowledge and reduce confusion. Checklists, policies and advanced guides have been prepared by Subject Matter Experts, open source practioners, and curated by our team, to make learning about using open source easy and efficient.

    You can find a catalogue of these resources at open source guide.
  • Open Source Champs: Our Community Discord channel makes it easy and straightforward to connect with open source maintainers and experts to get answers.

    The Open Source leaders come from teams across Tech companies in Africa and are able to help advise teams and help share expertise.

Standards OpenChain 2.1 conformance

We take trust very derio Trust is key to open source. Developers should be able to trust users to respect their licensing choices. And when you receive software, you should be able to trust that the open source licenses were followed.

The OpenChain Project plays a key role in building trust by setting standards that define how we operate a high-quality open source compliance program. It means that when you receive open source from a company that follows the OpenChain standard, you can be assured that the code went through a rigorous license compliance process. You can trust it.

We are working to become OpenChain 2.0-conformant by December 2024.
